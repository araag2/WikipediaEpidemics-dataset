A lack of clean drinking water in [[Iraq]] in 2007 led to an outbreak  of [[cholera]].<ref>{{cite news |title=U.N. reports cholera outbreak in northern Iraq |url=http://www.cnn.com/2007/WORLD/meast/08/29/iraq.cholera/index.html |publisher=CNN |date= 2007-08-30|access-date=2007-08-30 }}</ref> A total of approximately 7,000 people were infected, with 10 deaths reported.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.who.int/csr/don/2008_09_10a/en/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141003031604/http://www.who.int/csr/don/2008_09_10a/en/|url-status=dead|archive-date=October 3, 2014|title=Cholera in Iraq|publisher=WHO|date=10 September 2008|access-date=22 October 2017}}</ref>

According to Ryadh Abdul Ameer, the director of the Basra health ministry, basic water sterilization became impossible in some places due to restrictions on the availability of [[chlorine]] for water sterilization.<ref>{{cite news
