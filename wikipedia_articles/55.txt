In the '''2017 dengue outbreak in Peshawar''', [[Pakistan]], hundreds of incidents of [[dengue fever]] were reported in [[Peshawar]] in mid-2017.<ref name="Emergency meeting called over Peshawar dengue outbreak">{{cite web|url=https://www.dawn.com/news/1352536|publisher=www.dawn.com|title=Emergency meeting called over Peshawar dengue outbreak|date=19 August 2017}}</ref> Initially, according to the health department of [[Khyber Pakhtunkhwa]], 4,320 suspected cases were received by Khyber Teaching Hospital Peshawar and 831 were positive.<ref name="Emergency meeting called over Peshawar dengue outbreak" /> About a dozen people died of dengue, starting in July. Punjab healthcare experts and Peshawar experts worked together to control the dengue epidemic, using health workers going door-to-door to educate residents, as well as fumigation of the city to suppress mosquito populations.<ref name="Dengue continues to ring alarm bells in Peshawar">{{cite web|url=http://pakobserver.net/dengue-continues-ring-alarm-bells-peshawar/|publisher=pakobserver.net|title=Dengue continues to ring alarm bells in Peshawar|access-date=2017-08-24|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170824134227/http://pakobserver.net/dengue-continues-ring-alarm-bells-peshawar/|archive-date=2017-08-24|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="Dengue hits Peshawar">{{cite web|url=http://pakobserver.net/dengue-hits-peshawar/|publisher=pakobserver.net|title=Dengue hits Peshawar|access-date=2017-08-24|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170824235654/http://pakobserver.net/dengue-hits-peshawar/|archive-date=2017-08-24|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="Dengue experts to stay in Peshawar">{{cite web|url=https://www.dawn.com/news/1352949|publisher=www.dawn.com|title=Dengue experts to stay in Peshawar|date=21 August 2017}}</ref><ref name="Punjab dengue experts leave for Peshawar">{{cite web|url=http://reliefweb.int/report/pakistan/punjab-dengue-experts-leave-peshawar|publisher=reliefweb.int|title=Punjab dengue experts leave for Peshawar}}</ref>

In November 2017, the [[World Health Organization]] reported a total of 24,807 laboratory confirmed cases with 69 deaths.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://reliefweb.int/disaster/ep-2017-000133-pak|publisher=WHO|title=Pakistan: Dengue Outbreak - Sep 2017 |date= 16 December 2019 |accessdate= 15 May 2020 |website=reliefweb.int}}</ref>
